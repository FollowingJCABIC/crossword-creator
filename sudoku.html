How To Solve

Constraint-based reasoning beats scanning rows.

Looking for the most filled row or column is a good start, but strong Sudoku solving relies on consistent candidate pruning and logical patterns.

Core Strategy Lens

Every empty cell is the intersection of three constraint systems: row, column, and box. The goal is to reduce candidates at that intersection until one value remains.

8. Chain Reasoning (Intermediate)

Assume a candidate, follow consequences, and look for contradictions.

Example: if “5” fails, the other candidate must be true.

A Practical Solving Order

  1. Fill obvious singles.
  2. Update all pencil marks.
  3. Search for hidden singles.
  4. Check each box for pointing pairs.
  5. Look for naked pairs.
  6. Repeat the cycle.
